Major Description
  • ASTRONOMY AND ASTROPHYSICS
 Astronomy and Astrophysics at Villanova combines rigorous academic preparation with a strong research component, and prepares students for graduate studies, careers in science journalism and science education, and technically-based careers.
  • BIOCHEMISTRY
A BS in Biochemistry is excellent preparation for medical school or graduate work in the health sciences, pharmaceutical science, forensic science, food science, neurobiology and, of course, biochemistry and molecular biology.
  • BIOLOGY
 Biology at Villanova allows students to examine the integration and diversity of living systems from the subcellular to the ecosystem level, reach an understanding of fundamental biological principles and processes, and develop technical and problem solving skills.
  • CHEMISTRY
Chemistry faculty actively conduct research in diverse areas such as nanotechnology, environmental chemistry, natural product synthesis, catalysis, biochemistry, evolution and computational chemistry.
  • COMMUNICATION
Villanova offers eight major specializations—interpersonal communication, journalism, media production, media studies, organization communication, performance studies, public relations and rhetorical studies—and five certificates of graduate study in communications—Communication and Critical/Cultural Studies; Journalism and New Media; Mediated Communication; Organizational Communication and Leadership, and Public Relations and Advertising.
  • COMPUTER SCIENCE
 Villanova Computer Science faculty conduct research in an array of areas including: artificial intelligence, ransomware, big data analysis, wireless networks, virtual reality, nanocompilers, security and ethics, systems modeling and computational geometry.
  • CRIMINOLOGY
Criminology is an interdisciplinary program designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the social control systems of a society such as the justice system. Criminology is applicable to careers in social services, judiciary, law enforcement, business and education.
  • MATHEMATICS
The Mathematics program at Villanova prepares students to formalize, define, analyze, prove and communicate mathematical results.
  • PHILOSOPHY
The Philosophy Department at Villanova is committed to excellence in the teaching and study of philosophy, broadly conceived, with a primary emphasis on the history of philosophy, continental philosophy, social and political philosophy and ethics. Students engage in serious dialogue about ideas and concepts that shape the way we understand ourselves, the community and the world around us.
  • PYSCHOLOGY
Psychology is the basic and applied science of mind and behavior. Psychologists use the methods of both natural and social science to advance our knowledge and understanding of thought, emotion and behavior in humans and other organisms.
  • SOCIOLOGY
While everyone makes decisions, social structures and institutions affect and constrain those decisions, and Villanova’s Sociology students study the impact that the social environment has on human behavior.
